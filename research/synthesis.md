# Deep research synthesis (3 reports, July 2026)

Sources in `reports/`: (1) Strategic Evaluation, (2) Portfolio Research, (3) Strategy & Analysis. This doc records where they converge, where they conflict, and what changed in our operating plan as a result.

## Where all three converge (treat as settled)

**1. One flagship Lane 1B channel, sequenced — never simultaneous.** Report 3 quantifies it: ~35% failure rate sequenced vs ~90% simultaneous for solo operators. Noah Morris-style portfolios are team businesses ($70–600/video freelancers), not solo models. Second channel only after 12+ months AND benchmarks hit AND hired help.

**2. Email is the business; AdSense is the floor.** Starter Story: 75% of revenue from products, acquired by HubSpot Feb 2026. Codie Sanchez: $360K from YouTube in 2024 vs ~90% of income from products/PE on 2.2M subs. Report 3's funnel math: ~2% link CTR × ~15% opt-in ≈ 3 email subs per 1,000 views, worth $4.50–10.50/mo recurring per 1,000 views at $1.50–3.50 LTV/sub/mo — beats the ad RPM on the same views. Newsletter sponsor CPMs in B2B/operator niches: $50–200.

**3. Video-specific lead magnets, not a generic newsletter pitch.** Kit case studies: dedicated per-video magnets "significantly more effective"; topic-specific magnets converting up to 12%; one template asset drove 50K+ subscribers. Our blueprint-per-video format is exactly this.

**4. Policy-safe by design.** July 15, 2025 inauthentic-content policy + Jan 2026 enforcement wave. Safe profile = original research, own analysis/voice, custom charts, cited sources, disclosure toggle. Blueprint format sits at the safest end. Modern MBA markets "no AI-generated content" as a differentiator — worth copying as positioning language. Never: templated scripts with swapped nouns, unedited TTS, static AI slideshows, identical footprints across channels.

**5. The whitespace is confirmed and specific.** Nobody owns rigorous AI-company unit economics on video. Coverage is either listicles or text (Sacra/HBR). Report 3's search estimates: "how [AI company] makes money" 45–65K/mo, "[company] business model" 35–50K/mo, "how Cursor makes money" 5–9K/mo. Cursor is the ready-made flagship story: $100M ARR (Jan 2025) → ~$4B (June 2026), fastest SaaS ramp in history. (Caveat: "spends ~100% of revenue on compute" is reported, not confirmed; Sacra Apr 2026 says slight gross-margin profitability — present carefully.)

**6. Lane 2 lives inside the flagship.** Tutorial decay is brutal (40–60%/quarter for UI-led; concept/workflow videos last 18–24 months). So: "AI SDR workflow blueprint (problem → evidence → stack → n8n demo)", never "how to use n8n". Spin out a tutorial channel only if workflow episodes consistently win on watch time + affiliate clicks.

**7. Shorts = discovery only.** $0.01–0.45 RPM, ≤40% of uploads, pinned comment to the long-form (+20% documented watch-hour lift). Never a Shorts-first strategy.

**8. LinkedIn seeding mechanics (this changes our derivation spec).** External links penalized 50–70% reach; native documents/carousels are the top-engagement format (~7%). Correct motion: native LI document/carousel of the video's core framework + YT link in comments + newsletter send within the first hour of publish (early watch-time signal). An existing LI audience is a documented first-1,000-subs accelerator.

## Affiliate facts worth acting on (verified in reports, re-verify at publish)

ElevenLabs 22% recurring 12mo / 90-day cookie (best voice fit). Notion 50% / 12mo. Kit (ConvertKit) 50% recurring. HubSpot up to 30% / 180-day cookie (longest). beehiiv up to 50%. Jasper 25–30% but short cookie (14–45d, sources conflict). **Descript moved to $25 one-time** — no longer a recurring play; Pictory's own pages contradict each other. Rule: verify terms the week of publish; prefer recurring + long cookie. Documented aRPM in high-intent B2B tutorials: $3–10 per 1,000 views (one case: 120x the ad revenue on the same video).

## Changes made to the operating plan

1. **Cadence: 1 flagship/week** (was 2x/wk) — kill-criteria window restated as 26 weekly flagships + derivatives.
2. **Email infrastructure from video #1** (beehiiv or Kit; single-field opt-in — each added form field costs ~⅓ conversion). Capture gates added to kill criteria.
3. **LinkedIn derivation adds a native document/carousel** per video (top engagement format), YT links only in comments, newsletter within the first hour.
4. **Topic queue rebuilt** from the three reports' merged top-20 lists (`topics/queue.md`), scored per rubric. Pilot #1 = Cursor.
5. **Positioning line adopted:** original research, human-written analysis — say it publicly (Modern MBA precedent).
