# Competitive landscape synthesis (4 reports, July 2026)

Sources in `reports/`: comp-report-1 (Claude), comp-report-2 (ChatGPT), comp-report-3 (Gemini), comp-report-4 (PDF). Same drill as `synthesis.md`: convergence, conflicts, what changes in the plan.

## Where all four converge (treat as settled)

**1. The whitespace hypothesis is confirmed — but it's a combination gap, not a topic gap.** All four independently reach the same structure: the three required attributes each exist in the market — documentary rigor (MagnatesMedia, Modern MBA, How Money Works), buildable-business + real numbers (Starter Story, Codie, UpFlip), senior-safe register (Welsh, Bourgoin) — but never co-occur in one channel aimed at 35–55 skeptical operators. Report 2's framing is the most honest: "the promise is available, the audience is available, the design language is available, but the combination is not." Report 4 adds the time constraint: the window is "measured in quarters, not years."

**2. The register IS the moat.** Unanimous and the single most strategically important finding. Report 4: most incumbents *can't* reach LinkedIn-shareability 5 without abandoning the monetization model that funds them (course ladders, income-claim packaging). The gap is "economically defended" — evidence rigor is expensive and hard to monetize at small scale, which is why it's still open. The defensible asset is the consistent 5/5 tone, not the topic.

**3. The shareability pattern is stark and consistent.** Highest scores across all reports: Modern MBA, How Money Works, Justin Welsh, Katelyn Bourgoin, Sahil Bloom (5 or near). Lowest: Codie Sanchez (2–3), UpFlip (2–3), Dan Koe (2.5–3.5). The properties that tell you what to build score low; the properties that score high don't tell you what to build.

**4. HubSpot/Starter Story is threat #1.** All four name it first. 2.9M combined YT across Starter Story + The Hustle + MFM (+ Futurepedia per report 2). Early signal: Starter Story episodes shifting from "how I built" (retrospective interview) to "how you could build" (prospective + unit economics). Counter: their interview format can't easily produce standardized unit-economics blueprints — out-rigor them (report 1); plant the flag in the first 26 uploads so they're the imitator (report 4).

**5. Don't fake practitioner status; don't build a course funnel.** The audience actively polices both (Codie's r/FakeGuru threads are the cautionary tale). Phase 1 is honestly a media play — lead with sourced rigor, not "I did this." A $997-style CTA drops shareability from 5 toward 2. Blueprint lead magnet is the right, low-embarrassment capture; if/when monetizing, Starter Story's products/community model, never UpFlip's course-shilling.

**6. Media property with a visibly human host.** Reports 1/4 say "media property quadrant"; report 2 sharpens it: skeptical seniors trust either an accountable human with receipts or a media property that behaves like research — never a faceless guru-funnel. Resolution: a publication with a human host — "just to the personal side of Starter Story."

**7. Design consensus** — folded into `../brand/design-system.md`. Steal: MagnatesMedia motion/pacing, Justin Welsh restraint, Starter Story evidence framing, Growth in Reverse chart craft, Sahil Bloom editorial premium. Ban: income overlays, shocked faces, neon, glassmorphism, countdown timers, "rich friend secrets" copy.

## Report reliability notes

- **R1 (Claude):** best-sourced, most caveated; unique coverage of Modern MBA/How Money Works and the four-archetype split. Highest confidence.
- **R2 (ChatGPT):** best audience-sentiment mining (Reddit language) and expansion set (Futurepedia, Built Not Hired/Linara Bozieva, Ali Abdaal LBA, Simon Squibb, Dickie Bush). Trust the qualitative reads.
- **R3 (Gemini):** unique long-tail finds — the literal adjacent-name holders (Codner & Co./Exit Blueprint, Hire Yourself/Pete Gilfillan, Economy of One/Elizabeth Allen) and **Growth in Reverse** (Chenell Basilio) as a design/rigor reference. But several unverifiable specifics (Starter Story "68% YoY lead gen," "100M+ annual readers," invented math formulas) — treat its numbers as lowest-confidence, its discoveries as leads to verify.
- **R4 (PDF):** sharpest strategic framing (register-as-moat, economically-defended gap, "move before HubSpot"); sloppiest numbers (Codie 500K, UpFlip 5M).

## What changes in the operating plan

1. **Register discipline becomes a stated production gate.** Every episode/thumbnail/post passes the test: "would a 45-year-old VP share this under their real name?" Add to Gate 1 checklist.
2. **On-screen citation boxes** (Source: SEC filing / Sacra / named report) every time a number appears — the one thing the guru lane structurally can't copy (R3/R4, corroborates synthesis.md finding #4).
3. **Second-person, present-tense framing** is the wedge sentence vs. every format competitor: "here's the business *you could* build" vs. MagnatesMedia's history and Starter Story's "here's who did."
4. **Watch list (monthly, 5):** Starter Story (interview→prospective shift, production bump), MagnatesMedia (any present-tense "start this now" video), Codie/BizScout (register cleanup toward seniors), Greg Isenberg/Idea Browser (sourced unit economics added to the AI-idea engine), new faceless-documentary entrants pairing MagnatesMedia craft with Starter Story evidence (the playbook is being actively taught in 2026).
5. **Brand/design decisions** → `../brand/design-system.md`.
