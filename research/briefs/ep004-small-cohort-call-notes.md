## 1. Thesis pressure-test — the call changed the honest math

The queue thesis says "15 people × $2K is how independents monetize what they know." The on-record reality as of 7/14:

- **Joanie's current cohort: one enrollee** (PhD/professor-level clinician, heavy individualization, off the 8-week plan because he steers it).
- **Peer coach comparable: 6 enrollees at a similar price point.** Her $60 interview-coaching webinar also underfilled.
- **Buyer liquidity is the constraint:** clientele is unemployed clinicians; "$800 is a lot" in this market. Multiple consults recently, none converted. She's experimenting with finder's-fee / pay-on-placement structures.

**Refined framing (do NOT claim Joanie as the 15×$2K proof):** the economics of the small cohort work *because AI removed the two costs that used to make small numbers fatal* — platform fees (Podia/Maven/Kajabi tax) and manual ops. A cohort of six, or even one, no longer loses money while you build the audience. Maven's $25M a16z raise stays the high-end proof of market; Joanie is the low-end proof of **build cost ≈ zero**, not of demand. This is more honest and more differentiated than the fantasy version — it survives the channel's own evals.
