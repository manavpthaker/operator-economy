# YouTube craft & pre-publish rubric research

Three independent research reports on YouTube craft (hooks, titles, thumbnails,
retention, Shorts) and the 100-point **pre-publish content rubric** for The
Operator Economy, weighted to the 2025–2026 post-AI-fatigue landscape. They
converge on the same core findings, so read them as one evidence base with
three levels of citation rigor.

## The five hard rules all three agree on

1. Thesis/premise spoken by **0:15**, promise fully cashed by **0:30**.
2. Title + thumbnail + first 30s function as **one promise-delivery unit** (native A/B now picks winners by watch time, not CTR).
3. Thumbnail communicates **one visual idea**, ≤3–4 words, high contrast, legible at mobile size.
4. **No CTA before the first payoff**; lead-magnet CTA lands mid-video (≈55–75% runtime) after a value peak.
5. Ship **authenticity markers** — human voice, on-screen sourcing, material variation — to stay on the safe side of the July 15 2025 inauthentic-content policy.

## Applying it

The rubric is a **pre-publish gate**: score every script + package (title,
thumbnail, description) before upload — suggested gate ≥80/100 with zero
kill-list items. Then calibrate the weights against your own 24h/7d/28d
analytics over the first ~20 videos (see each report's post-publish table).
This feeds the production pipeline in `../../docs/pipeline.md` (Gates 1–3) and
the topic scoring in `../../topics/scoring.md`.

> Source caveat: official YouTube statements and peer-reviewed findings are
> high-confidence; specific retention/CTR percentages from SEO-tool blogs and
> vendor studies are directional. The post-publish validation tables exist to
> test them against real channel data.
