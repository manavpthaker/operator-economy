# Competitive landscape deep research prompt

Paste into a deep research tool (or ask Claude to run it). Companion to `deep-research-prompt.md` (lane validation) — this one maps the competitive set itself: the people, audiences, content, and brand execution.

---

I'm launching "The Operator Economy" — a YouTube channel + newsletter for senior professionals (35–55, product/marketing/ops leadership) leaving the broken job market. Format: thesis-led episodes ("this is the kind of business you can build"), real companies as evidence spanning solo-operator to venture-scale, every video ships a downloadable blueprint lead magnet. Voice: documentary rigor, no hype, sourced numbers — New Yorker restraint with MagnatesMedia narrative structure. Tagline: "Build. Own. Operate."

I need an intensive competitive analysis of everyone operating in or adjacent to this space. Prioritize 2025–2026 data and primary sources (the channels/sites themselves, Social Blade, archived pages, creator interviews and earnings disclosures, podcast appearances) over SEO listicles.

## 1. Define the competitive set

Start from this seed list, then expand it: **Starter Story (acquired by HubSpot), Greg Isenberg / Late Checkout, Codie Sanchez / Contrarian Thinking, My First Million (Shaan Puri & Sam Parr), UpFlip, MagnatesMedia, Noah Kagan, Pat Walls, Justin Welsh, Dan Koe, Katelyn Bourgoin, The Hustle/Trends alumni projects**, plus whoever currently owns adjacent naming territory ("Built Not Hired", "Hire Yourself", "Economy of One", "Exit Blueprint" — all taken by people with adjacent positioning).

For expansion: (a) find 10–15 more channels/newsletters serving "corporate professional → owner/operator" transitions, "business idea + playbook" content, or AI-business-opportunity content; (b) classify each as **direct** (same audience + same promise), **adjacent** (same audience, different promise — e.g., career pivot coaches, solopreneur productivity), or **format** competitors (same content shape, different audience — e.g., finance documentary channels). Note anyone who launched in the last 12 months — the newest entrants show where the space is heading.

## 2. The people

For each direct and adjacent competitor: Who is the face? Career background and what credibility claim they lead with (exited founder, ex-operator, media person, anonymous/faceless?). On-camera presence: full face, voiceover-only, interview host, animated? Team size if discoverable (solo, small studio, funded media company). How do they establish trust in the first 60 seconds of a video and above the fold on their site? What's their origin story as they tell it, and has it been challenged or fact-checked? Which of them are practitioners of what they preach vs. pure media businesses about the topic — and does the audience notice or care (check comments/Reddit)?

## 3. The audience

For each: Who actually watches/reads (demographics where discoverable, but especially psychographics — aspiration level, sophistication, tolerance for hype)? Mine YouTube comments, Reddit threads, and community spaces for the audience's own language: what do they praise, what do they complain about, what do they say is missing? Where does each audience gather off-platform (Discord, Skool, Circle, newsletter replies)? Critically: **which competitors serve hustle-hungry 20-somethings vs. skeptical senior professionals?** My audience is allergic to hustle-bro content and must be able to share videos under their real name on LinkedIn without embarrassment — score each competitor 1–5 on that "LinkedIn shareability" test and justify the score.

## 4. The content

For each: formats used (documentary, talking head, screen-share, interview, listicle), episode length, upload cadence, and how these changed over the last 18 months. Title and thumbnail patterns — hooks used, income claims vs. evidence framing, faces vs. graphics. Narrative structure of a representative video (cold open? thesis? case-study stack? CTA placement?). Evidence standards: do they source numbers, estimate loosely, or make unsourced income claims? How do they handle AI topics — as tooling, as business opportunity, or not at all? What's their lead magnet / content upgrade strategy per video? Cross-platform derivation: how do they repurpose to LinkedIn, Shorts, newsletter, and which direction does their funnel flow?

## 5. Brand, design, look and feel

For each competitor with a real web presence: describe the site's visual identity — typography, color palette, density, photography vs. illustration, overall register (premium editorial? startup landing page? creator-economy default?). Same for their YouTube packaging: thumbnail art direction, in-video motion graphics quality, intro/outro treatment, music choices. Newsletter design and voice. Does the brand feel like a *media property* or a *personal brand*? Which brands would a 45-year-old VP of Product perceive as credible vs. embarrassing, and what specific design choices create that perception? Identify the 2–3 best-designed properties in the set and what specifically they do (with URLs/examples I can study), and the common "creator-economy default" tropes to avoid.

## 6. Business model and scale

For each: estimated revenue mix (ads, sponsors, affiliate, courses/community, newsletter, exits) with sources. Subscriber counts, average views per video (last 90 days), growth trajectory over 24 months, engagement rates. Email list size where disclosed and how they capture. What did the ones who scaled do at my stage (first 26 uploads)? What do stalled or abandoned channels in this space have in common?

## 7. Similarities, differences, whitespace

Synthesize: (a) a positioning map (axes: evidence rigor ↔ hype, personal brand ↔ media property; plot everyone including where The Operator Economy should sit); (b) a similarity/difference matrix — for each direct competitor, what we overlap on, what genuinely differentiates us, and what they do better that we should learn from; (c) whitespace analysis — which audience + promise + format combinations are unserved? Specifically test my hypothesis: **nobody is serving skeptical senior operators with documentary-grade, sourced, no-hype "here's the business you could build" content with real unit economics.** Confirm or kill it with evidence; (d) threats — who is best positioned to move into my lane, and what would early signs look like?

## 8. Output

Deliver: (1) a competitor table (name, type, face/format, audience, cadence, subs/views, revenue mix, LinkedIn-shareability score, one-line differentiation vs. me); (2) the positioning map; (3) a brand/design reference sheet — what to steal, what to avoid, with specific examples; (4) the top 5 strategic implications for a channel launching now at 1 video/week; (5) a "watch list" of the 5 competitors to monitor monthly and what signal to watch for.

---

**Context for the researcher:** This is a pure media play (break-even-plus goal, ~$100–300/mo costs, 8–10 hrs/wk, 26-week commitment). Phase 2 adds practitioner interviews. The differentiator is evidence rigor + human POV in an AI-content-flooded niche — the analysis should stress-test whether that differentiation is real and visible to the target audience.
