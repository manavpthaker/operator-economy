# Tactical Shift — the automated retention-consulting practice
### Blueprint v0 · draft for Joanie · July 2026

**The thesis:** You already built the hard part. The assessments exist, the website is live, the methodology is in your spreadsheets, and you've run one engagement end to end. What's missing is the layer that turns raw assessment data into client-ready deliverables without you rebuilding a slide deck by hand every time. That layer is now cheap to build. The distribution is still on you — no tool fixes that — but the pitch gets much easier when the product behind it runs itself.

**The math that sells this:** losing one clinician costs a practice ~$120K/year in revenue. Lose five and a small practice can't cover operating costs. Against that, a $5–7.5K engagement isn't a cost, it's insurance — and it's a fraction of the $150K full-time HR hire that's the only alternative anyone offers them. Your target from our call: 2–3 clients a year ≈ $20–30K. That's the honest number. This is a strong second income stream, not a startup.

## Phase 1 — Automate the delivery, keep it invisible (weeks 1–2, before any client)

Do not build a platform yet. Build a pipeline you run yourself.

1. **Standardize intake.** One template for what a client hands you: assessment exports, headcount, tenure data, turnover history, org structure. If it isn't in the template, you don't accept it. This single decision is what makes automation possible.
2. **Set up a Claude project loaded with your methodology.** Your spreadsheets, your scoring logic, an anonymized sample of the pilot engagement, your deliverable templates. This becomes your analyst.
3. **Build the four generators** (prompt + template each, I'll help set these up):
   - Engagement dashboard — one self-contained HTML page per client: baseline scores, pain points, attrition risk, trends once re-measures exist
   - 3-month strategic plan — actions assigned to named leaders, sequenced
   - Individual profiles — per-leader communication and engagement guidance from the individual assessments
   - The client deck — branded, generated from the above, not rebuilt in Canva
4. **Dry-run it on the pilot data.** Your friend's (anonymized) engagement goes through the pipeline start to finish. What comes out the other side is your sales demo.

Cost of Phase 1: a Claude subscription and some setup time. No code you have to maintain, nothing to host.

## Phase 2 — Productize (after the first paying client, not before)

Once a real client has been through Phase 1, we build the Reframe & Ready version: a client portal where their dashboard lives and updates, re-assessment cycles tracked over time, automated check-in emails. Same stack as R&R, so the build is fast and the pattern is proven. The trigger for Phase 2 is a paying client asking to log in — not a hunch.

## Phase 3 — The engagement itself (what you sell)

**"The fast track to retention" — 90 days, $5–7.5K:**

- Weeks 1–2: baseline. Assessments run, data through the pipeline, dashboard + strategic plan delivered in days, not weeks. (This speed IS the credibility moment.)
- Weeks 3–10: the human part. Leadership training, the hard conversations, working the plan with their leaders. This is the part they're actually paying for, and nothing here gets automated.
- Weeks 11–12: re-measure, updated dashboard, handoff plan. The before/after trend line is your case study and their renewal reason.

First client: price it as a founding engagement (discounted, or your friend's practice done fully) in exchange for a named case study and the trend-line data.

## Distribution — the part no blueprint automates

Three warm lanes, in order:

1. **Your own network of practice owners and administrators.** You were one of them. The $120K line is the opener; the 48-hour dashboard is the proof.
2. **The home-health app relationship.** Same buyers, adjacent problem. If that consulting formalizes, retention work is a natural add-on for their customers.
3. **LinkedIn**, but only once 1 and 2 have produced a case study. Content writes itself from real engagements: "here's what a practice's engagement data looked like 90 days apart."

## What stays human (say this in every pitch)

The automation does intake, analysis, dashboards, decks, and tracking. You do the leadership training, the interpretation, the judgment calls, and the trust. Clients aren't buying software from you — they're buying a consultant whose backend happens to be faster than everyone else's.
