# Manav speech profile — extracted from the 2026-07-04 recording session

Source of truth for how the narrator ACTUALLY talks. The performance pass

## Sentence mechanics

- NO EM DASHES. Ever. His clauses either restart as new sentences, run on
  with "and", or break with an ellipsis/pause. Where a writer wants "—",
  he does one of: new sentence · "..." · paragraph break.
- Short declaratives stacked, not subordinate clauses:
  "You're not doing that. You're buying proof."
- "Period." as an intensifier after a rule: "Proof is the only marketing
  this business needs. Period."
- Sentence starts he actually uses: "Okay so" · "And look" · "Look" ·
  "All right, let's talk about" · "Here's the thing" · "But here's the
  detail that matters more" · "That's" / "This is" (deictic starts,
  constantly) · "And that's" · "So".

## Signature moves (use these, they ARE the voice)

1. ASK-THEN-ANSWER: he poses the question and immediately answers it.
   "What's the gap? What's the gap between these businesses knowing what
   they need and actually doing it?" · "And what is that end? The end is:
   I'm going to make your missed calls get answered and booked."
2. NEGATE-THEN-REPLACE: kill the wrong frame before giving the right one.
   "That's not a vertical. That's a cop-out." · "Nobody wants automation.
   That's not what they're looking for. That is the means to an end."
   · "It's not a transaction anymore."
3. DOUBLE-TAP LANDING: repeat the payoff with a small variation.
   "That's the sentence and that's the solution." · "That's the
   relationship. That's the communication part of it."
4. ANAPHORA TRIPLETS: "It's intake, it's follow-ups, it's reporting." ·
   "helping the team through it, helping the company through it."
5. OPEN SELF-REPAIR (sparingly, 1-2 per episode): correct and keep moving.
   "Six years. Not six years, sorry, take that back. Over a decade."
6. PREEMPT THE OBJECTION: "And before you question doing free work..."
7. SECTION PIVOTS AS CONVERSATION: "Okay. This is all well and good,
   right? But how does the math work out? Let's talk about it."
8. APHORISM/WISECRACK (max one per episode): "A fool with a tool is
   still a fool."

## Vocabulary

- Money: "two grand", "five to six grand a month", "20 bucks", "bucks",
  "billion with a B", "spring for".
- Casual verbs: "stand this up", "wire them into", "pull through",
  "snowballs", "round it down to zero".
- Tags: "right?" mid-flow · "You know" occasionally · "Okay" as a
  paragraph reset.
- Direct address: "you", "your" everywhere; talks TO one person.

## Colloquial register (Manav, 2026-07-04: "has to feel like just
## talking this out as I go")

- CONTRACT EVERYTHING natural speech contracts: "wanna", "gonna",
  "gotta", "kinda", "let's", "that's", "it's", "you're", "don't".
  Never "want to / going to" where a person would say wanna/gonna.
- Numbers land conversationally: "five nine billion... that's billion
  with a B", "two grand", "20 bucks a month, maybe".
- His hedge-and-commit move: "or whatever you wanna call it", "for
  lack of a better term", "I don't know, maybe they do in an abstract
  sense... but", then lands the point anyway.
- Working-it-out asides (1–2 short ones per section, MORE than the old
  profile allowed): "and before you go 'wait, free work?'...", "this
  is not a hey-I'm-on-a-job-interview homework situation".
- Casual dismissals: "none of that", "that's too broad, too generic",
  "that's a cop-out", "That's it. That's the ask."
- It should read like the ad-libbed parts of his session transcript —
  the thousand unscripted words are the register target, not the
  written script.
