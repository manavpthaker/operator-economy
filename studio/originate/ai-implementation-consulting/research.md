# Research brief: "You can sell AI implementation to businesses that are drowning in it"

Pilot episode #1. Thesis: businesses are buying AI implementation help at every scale — from $2K solo projects to $5.9B enterprise bookings — and the service model (not building AI, but installing it) is the most accessible AI business for an experienced operator.

## Evidence — HIGH END (confidence: HIGH, primary/press sources)

- **Accenture FY2025 (ended Aug 31, 2025): $5.9B in GenAI bookings (~2x YoY), $2.7B GenAI revenue (~3x YoY).** Q1 FY2026: $2.2B advanced-AI bookings, +76% YoY; Accenture stopped isolating AI metrics because AI now touches ~80% of large deals. Source: CIO Dive, Constellation Research, Accenture FY2025 annual report.
- GenAI bookings grew even while Accenture's overall new bookings were flat — AI implementation is offsetting decline in traditional consulting. Source: Constellation Research.
- Market signal: ~1 in 2 GenAI/agentic projects pulls through data-modernization work (services attach to services). Source: CIO Dive.

## Evidence — LOW END (confidence: MIXED — creator-economy blogs, mark as reported/unverified)

- Reported model: solo AI automation agency at ~$40K/mo from 5 clients on $5K retainers + performance fees, ~$6K/mo costs → ~85% margins. Source: Medium (The AI Studio, Mar 2026) — UNVERIFIED, present as "reported."
- Reported freelancer floor: AI automation freelancer at ~$6K/mo on ~$20/mo tool costs. Source: Medium — UNVERIFIED anecdote.
- Typical pricing ranges (multiple sources converge): $2,000–$5,000 initial SMB projects; $5K–$50K per automation project; $500–$5,000/mo retainers. Confidence: MEDIUM (consistent across sources, no audited data).
- Deep-research report 1 (in ../../research/reports/) lists AI implementation consulting with "documented ~90% margins" — treat as directional; cost structure logic (labor + <$100/mo tools) supports high margins.
- Cost-compression anchor: a 7-agent Claude automation system at ~$74–84/mo API cost replacing ~$7–8K/mo of labor (Medium, unverified but illustrative of the mechanism).

## Manav's POV material (Gate 1 will inject)

- Built and sold a hospitality tech product (Subziwalla exit); Head of Product roles; currently runs Grapevines (AI career tools) and automated content pipelines — has personally sold/installed AI-adjacent product into ops-heavy businesses.
- Hospitality angle: hotels/restaurants are the canonical "drowning in it" buyer — legacy PMS/POS, thin margins, labor shortage.

## Tool stack (for stack section; verify affiliate terms at publish)

Claude/ChatGPT (analysis + build, ~$20-40/mo) · n8n or Make (automation runtime; n8n self-hosted = near-zero) · Zapier (simpler installs) · Airtable/Notion (client-facing systems; Notion affiliate 50%/12mo) · Loom (async client comms). Total tooling < $100/mo.

## Playbook skeleton (for playbook section)

1. Pick one vertical you've operated in (multiple sources recommend vertical focus: real estate, e-comm, law, hospitality).
2. Build 2–3 free/discounted case studies (no-code: Make/Zapier/n8n).
3. Package as fixed-scope projects ($2–5K) → convert to retainers ($500–5K/mo).
4. First customer: your existing network/LinkedIn — operators trust operators.

## Failure modes / honesty notes (for economics section)

- Service business ≠ passive: revenue stops when you stop. Retainers mitigate, don't eliminate.
- The Medium case studies are marketing artifacts; realistic solo ramp is likely $2–8K/mo in year one, not $40K.
- Client concentration risk at 3–5 clients; enterprise sales cycles kill solo operators — stay SMB.
- Platform risk: automations break when tools change (maintenance is the moat AND the treadmill).
