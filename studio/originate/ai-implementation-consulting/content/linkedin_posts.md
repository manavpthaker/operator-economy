# LinkedIn posts: The $5.9 Billion Business You Can Start for $100

**Theme: The Broken Market**

Accenture's overall new bookings were flat last year.

One line item doubled: generative AI work. $5.9 billion of it — and by late 2025 they stopped reporting it separately, because AI touched 80% of large deals.

Here's what that means if you're a senior professional wondering where the ground is:

The market isn't paying for AI. It's paying for *installation* — someone who stands there until the thing actually runs.

Companies buy that at $5.9B scale from Accenture. Small businesses buy the exact same service at $2,000 scale from a single operator.

Same gap. Same service. Three price points.

The skills you already have — running systems, translating between technical and operational — are the qualification. (Sources in comments.)

---

**Theme: Building with AI**

The full tool stack for an AI implementation business costs less than $100 a month.

Claude or ChatGPT for the brain: $20–40. n8n self-hosted for the runtime: roughly free. Airtable or Notion for the client dashboard. Loom for delivery.

Cost of goods on a $2,000 project: effectively zero.

Which sounds like the whole story, and isn't. The tooling was never the hard part. The hard part is the same thing it's always been — a first client who trusts you.

That's why the playbook starts with the industry you've already worked in, not with the software. Operators buy from operators.

---

**Theme: Career as Product**

I spent six years building product for hospitality businesses. The pattern I saw everywhere: technology that got bought, half-installed, and quietly abandoned.

The gap was never the software. It was the person who'd stand there until it actually ran.

That person is now a business model. Fixed-scope installs, $2–5K. Maintenance retainers after. Every install surfaces the next problem — the reservation fix exposes the inventory mess, the inventory fix exposes the reporting mess.

That's not scope creep. That's the model.

If you've operated inside an industry for years, you can name its broken intake processes from memory. That memory is the asset.
