# Operator Blueprint #001 — AI Implementation Services

*The sourced working document behind the episode. Act on it without watching. Every number carries its source; estimates are marked.*

## The idea

Businesses at every scale are paying other people to **install** AI — not build it. The service: take tools that already exist (Claude/GPT, n8n/Make/Zapier, Airtable/Notion) and wire them into how one specific industry's businesses actually run — intake, follow-ups, reporting. Fixed-scope projects that convert to maintenance retainers.

## The playbook

1. **Week 1 — pick the industry you can argue with.** One vertical you've worked in, whose problems you can name from memory. Not "small businesses."
2. **Weeks 2–4 — buy proof.** Build 2–3 real case studies, cheap or free, for businesses in your network. Proof is the only marketing this business needs.
3. **Package one named problem.** Fixed scope, $2–5K, one deadline. Businesses don't buy "automation" — they buy "your missed calls get answered and booked."
4. **First client = your network.** Operators buy from operators; your credibility from the industry you left is the asset.
5. **Convert to retainer.** Maintenance + one new workflow/month, $500–5K by scope. The first project is designed to surface the second (the attach effect).

## The honest math

Costs: tools under $100/mo plus your time — that's how the ~90% margin claims happen (directionally right; Deep-research report 1). **Realistic year one: $2–8K/mo — our estimate**, not the $40K/mo stories, which are marketing artifacts from people selling courses about this exact business.

**Failure modes:** (1) it's a service — revenue stops when you stop; retainers soften, don't cure; (2) at 3–5 clients, losing one is a quarter of your income; (3) every automation you install is something you now maintain when the tools update. Stay SMB — enterprise sales cycles kill solo operators.
