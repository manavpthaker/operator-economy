## Post copy (paste-ready)

A 2024 study found only 38% of calls to small businesses get answered by a live person. One study, 85 businesses, vendor-adjacent, so hold it loosely. But everyone I've asked who has run a front desk says the shape is right.

There's a business forming in that gap: solo operators selling "we answer your phones" to local service businesses with AI voice agents. The tools run under $150 a month before usage. Clients pay $300 to $700 a month. Honest year-one read is $1.5K to $5K a month from a handful of local clients, and that's an estimate, not a promise.

Here's the part I can't settle, and I'd take this group's read over my own. The platforms making this possible are already moving up the stack. Bland raised prices once. Retell sells an AI receptionist direct to businesses. So if you were building this as a product of one, which is the real position: the reselling layer, riding the window while it's open and banking the client relationships, or skipping the middleman play entirely and owning a vertical relationship where the vendor underneath is swappable?

Breakdown attached. Source: The Operator Economy.
