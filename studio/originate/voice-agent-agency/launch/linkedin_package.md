## 1. Episode post — Mon 2026-07-13, 11:00 AM ET (attach carousel-002.pdf LAST, then Schedule)

A plumber misses 15 calls a week. At $350 a job, that's over $270,000 a year in work that rang, went to voicemail, and called the next name on the list.

That math is arithmetic, not a study. The study part: one 2024 survey found only 38% of calls to small businesses get answered by a live person. Single vendor-adjacent study, so treat it as reported, not gospel. The episode flags it that way too.

Episode № 002 breaks down the business in that gap: selling "we answer your phones" to local service businesses with AI voice agents. Who pays, the stack (under $150 a month before usage), how solo operators price it, and where it breaks: proper nouns, angry callers, platforms raising prices under you.

One thing the research didn't find: a verified $40K-a-month solo case study. The voice-agent version of that story doesn't exist yet. Honest year-one range is $1.5K to $5K a month from a handful of local clients, and that's an estimate. The shaky numbers stay flagged as shaky.
