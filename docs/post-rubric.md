# The Post Rubric

**Every LinkedIn post, carousel copy, group prompt, and DM runs through this before it ships.** Adapted July 6, 2026 from the brown-man-content voice system (the battle-tested Grapevines rules), re-tuned for The Operator Economy's register: documentary rigor, operator credibility, evidence-first, never hype. The audience is 35–55 ops/product leaders — they detect synthetic content and marketing register instantly, and the register is the moat.

---

## 1. Hooks (the first line decides everything)

**OE hook grammar — open with one of these:**
- **The number under tension:** "Accenture booked $5.9 billion doing something that also exists as a $2,000 solo project."
- **The contradiction:** "Accenture's overall bookings were flat last year. One line doubled."
- **The fact-check:** "There's a number making the rounds: $40K/month. I checked it."
- **The uncomfortable mechanic:** "The market isn't paying for AI. It's paying for installation."
- **The specific scene** (personal analyst posts only): "Every install my team ever did surfaced three problems we weren't hired to fix."

**Banned openers:**
- "Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:" (meme-tier AI LinkedIn)
- "Most [X] starts with the wrong [Y]" reframe openers
- Grand thesis statements with no number or scene in the first two lines
- Questions as hooks ("What if you could...?") — OE asserts, then earns it
- Anything that could open a guru funnel: "Here's how to make $X..."

**Hook test:** would a skeptical operator stop scrolling because the *fact* is surprising — not because the promise is big?

## 2. LLM watermark ban (applies to EVERYTHING: posts, carousel copy, comments, DMs, newsletter)

**Punctuation:**
- NO em dashes (—) in LinkedIn post bodies. Commas, periods, or rewrite. (#1 AI tell. Carousel slides and docs may use them; feed copy may not.)
- No excessive ellipses, no random bold mid-paragraph.

**Dead giveaway words (never):** delve, tapestry, testament, multifaceted, cutting-edge, revolutionary, pivotal, meticulous, intricate, vibrant, robust, nuanced, holistic, synergy, leverage (as verb), utilize, foster, garner, underscore, bolster, illuminate, facilitate, harness, seamlessly, landscape (metaphorical), realm, beacon, endeavor, embark, moreover, furthermore, additionally, certainly, indeed, notably.

**Dead giveaway phrases (never):** "I'd love to..." / "really resonates" / "in today's ever-evolving..." / "it's important to note..." / "harness the power of..." / "game-changing" / "this is a testament to..." / "empowering people to..." / "unlocking the potential of..." / "Think [x], [y], and [z]" / "whether you're [x] or [y]" closers.

**OE-specific additions (never):** "AI-powered" (describe what it does), "passive income", "side hustle", any income promise or "you could be making...", "hustle/grind/hack/easy/simple/instant", "best/#1", superlatives about the channel itself.

**Structural tells to break:**
- Perfect parallel lists (three items all shaped "Not X. The actual Y.")
- Clean antithetical constructions and mirror-image closers ("You stop X. You start Y.")
- One-line engagement-bait closers ("And that changes everything.")
- Every paragraph 1–2 sentences of equal length — real writing is uneven
- The same post structure twice in a row (problem → reframe → framework → closer)

**Sentence rhythm (the #1 giveaway per real feedback):**
- Mix 4-word sentences with 30-word ones. If every sentence is 12–18 words, rewrite.
- Never start consecutive sentences the same way.
- Break subject-verb-object monotony: lead with a clause, a prepositional phrase, or drop the subject.
- Let one thought run long with commas and "and" because it's being worked out in real time. Then stop short.
- Tap test: read it and tap per sentence. Metronome = rewrite.

## 3. Register rules by surface

| Surface | Voice | "I" allowed | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| OE page posts | The publication. Confident, spare, evidence-first. "Episode № 001 breaks down..." | No (the page is not a person) | "In the comments" only |
| Personal analyst posts | Manav analyzing the material. Peer, not guru. | Yes — required (a real observation or experience per post) | OE cited neutrally as a source (like CIO Dive). NEVER claimed as his, NEVER performed as a discovery ("came across this gem") — no origin story either way |
| Group prompts | Host asking a genuine question | Yes | Episode as reference material, question is the point |
| DMs | Friend who saw something relevant | Yes | No ask. Ever. |

**Universal register rules:**
- Every number gets a confidence flag when it deserves one: verified / reported / estimate. "The shaky numbers are flagged as shaky" IS the brand.
- Never name competitors or other creators negatively.
- Validate the reader's skepticism; never exploit fear or FOMO. No fake urgency, no scarcity.
- Contractions always. Fragments are fine.
- If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it. If it sounds like a press release, delete it.

## 4. Pre-publish sniff test (run on EVERY post, 60 seconds)

1. **Read it out loud.** Page post: does it sound like a sharp research publication, or like LinkedIn content? Personal post: does it sound like Manav in conversation?
2. **Cover the byline.** Could literally any AI-business account have written this? If yes, it's missing the specific number, scene, or fact-check that only this research produced.
3. **Check for symmetry.** Parallel constructions, mirror closers, templated lists → break them.
4. **Check the hook** against §1: banned openers, fact-before-promise.
5. **Check the flags.** Any number without a confidence level that needs one?
6. **Tap out the rhythm.** Even spacing = rewrite.
7. **The guru test (OE-specific).** Would this post survive being screenshot into r/FakeGuru? If any line promises outcomes, hypes the channel over the finding, or performs discovery of OE ("stumbled onto," "found this channel") — cut it. Neutral citation only; no origin story in either direction.

## 5. Ship gate

- All seven sniff-test checks pass → ship.
- Any check fails → revise, don't rationalize. The register compounds; one off-register post costs more than a missed slot.
- When in doubt between two versions, ship the one with the smaller claim and the better-sourced number.
