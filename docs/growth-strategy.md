# Growth Strategy (the layer on top of the publishing flow)

**`publishing-flow.md` gets each episode to people we already have. This doc is about people we don't.** Three levers, in expected-payoff order. Everything here fits inside the 8–10 hrs/wk total budget — the growth layer gets ~2–3 hrs/wk, and every touchpoint passes `post-rubric.md`. Window context: all of this serves the 26-week kill-criteria gates (views, subs/mo, AVD, capture rate) — leading indicators per lever are at the bottom.

---

## Lever 1 — Comment-led acquisition (LinkedIn + YouTube TOF)

The mechanic: thoughtful analyst-register comments on posts/videos where the ICP (35–55 ops/product leaders in transition) already gathers. A comment that adds a sourced number or a sharp fact-check earns profile visits → follows → OE trail. Same motion the linkedin-outreach engine runs for Grapevines; OE needs its own target list.

**Rules of engagement:**
- 5–10 comments/day max, ≤20 min. Comment because the *fact* adds something; never link-drop, never mention OE unprompted. The profile is the funnel (headline + featured section carry the trail).

- Never comment critically on a watch-list competitor (rubric §3: never name competitors negatively).
- Track which targets convert (profile visits → follows) monthly; rotate the bottom third out.

## Lever 2 — Search-optimized packaging (the post-1K engine)

LinkedIn ignites the first 1,000 subs; **YouTube search + suggested is the only lever that scales past it** (synthesis #5: validated demand — "how [AI company] makes money" 45–65K/mo, "[company] business model" 35–50K/mo, "how Cursor makes money" 5–9K/mo).

**Per-episode packaging checklist (run at Phase 1 scheduling):**
1. Title targets one validated query phrasing; entity name front-loaded ("How Cursor Makes Money — ..." not "The Wild Story of...").
2. Description: first two lines restate the query naturally + one number-under-tension. Episode + blueprint links above the fold.
3. Thumbnail = title-card frame (design-system rule); test: readable at 120px, zero hype elements (ban list in `../brand/design-system.md`).
4. Chapters on every episode (search surfaces chapter results; AVD diagnostics come free).
5. SRT uploaded (accessibility + indexing).
6. Pinned comment: blueprint link + the sources list (the citation box habit, in comments form).
7. End screen: Subscribe + "best for viewer" (already in publishing flow).
8. Log target query per episode in `../topics/queue.md` at scoring time — demand ÷ competition already drives the queue; packaging just has to cash the check.

**Suggested-feed second order:** the first 26 episodes should interlink (evidence segments reference prior episodes; end screens chain theses). Suggested traffic compounds only if the library is a web, not a list.

## Lever 3 — The amplifier flywheel (Tier 2 DMs → collaborators → interviews)

Tier 2 DM recipients (operators/amplifiers, 3–5/episode per publishing flow) who *engage* become collaboration candidates. The ladder:

1. **Reaction** — they reply/comment on the thesis. Log it.
2. **Quote** — their reaction appears in a future episode or post, credited ("an operator who runs this exact model pushed back: ..."). Costs nothing, flatters honestly, seeds reciprocal sharing.
3. **Research source** — they fact-check a number or supply real economics for an episode in their domain. Named citation on screen (the citation-box moat, now with living sources).
4. **Interview** — Phase 2 (upload 12–26 per positioning doc). Topic #14 (expertise-as-product) is the designed interview seed. Guests bring audiences; every interview is framed as *their* creation, their credit.

**Tracker** (add to relationship notes; per-episode review at DM-shortlist time): name, segment (operator/amplifier/ICP), episode sent, reaction, ladder stage, next step. The DM-shortlist generator (publishing-flow backlog #5) should read and write this.

**Product of One group as research instrument:** member answers to group prompts feed episode research (already in publishing flow). Members quoted in episodes (with permission) are amplifiers by definition — the group is the warmest rung of this ladder.

## What we deliberately don't do

No paid promotion (budget guardrail + the register: an ad for documentary rigor is self-refuting). No engagement pods, no follow-back games. No cross-promo begging — collaboration flows through the amplifier ladder or not at all. No Shorts-first pivots chasing views ($0.01–0.45 RPM, discovery only). No comment automation — one detected synthetic comment costs the moat.

## Leading indicators (monthly review, alongside kill-criteria analytics)

| Lever | Leading indicator | Healthy signal |
|---|---|---|
| Comments | LI profile visits + follows/wk; YT comment likes | Follows trending up; ≥1 inbound DM/wk by month 3 |
| Search packaging | Impressions + CTR per episode (YT Studio), search % of traffic | Search % rising by episode 8–12; CTR ≥4% |
| Amplifiers | Ladder stages advanced/mo; quoted reactions per episode | ≥1 stage-2+ move per episode by month 3 |
| All | Email capture rate (the kill-criteria metric that matters) | ≥0.5% floor, >1% = format proven |

Rebalance effort toward whichever lever's indicator moves first. If none move by upload 12, the archetype review (kill-criteria checkpoint) is the forum — growth tactics don't override format findings.
